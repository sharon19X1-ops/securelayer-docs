Phase 1 Demo — Talk Track

Beat-by-beat script for walking an MSSP prospect through the demo. About 4 minutes. Open the demo on one screen, keep this on the other. DO = what to click. SAY = what to say.

1 Connect
2 Discover
3 Score
4 Splunk
5 Enforce
6 Audit
Before you start~10s

Frame it

Say"This is SecureLayer running as your MSSP console, managing one of your SMB clients — BlueSky Software. Nothing is installed on their machines. Let me show you what we find and what you can do about it."
Say"AI coding agents — Claude Code, Cursor, Copilot, MCP servers — are running in every client you manage. None of your current tools can see what they're allowed to do. That's the gap."
Beat 1 — Connect & Scan~45s

Onboard a client

DO · Click "1 Connect"
Say"Onboarding a client takes minutes. You authorize read-only access to their GitHub org — OAuth, no credentials stored, nothing pushed to endpoints."
Say"We link the RMM you already run — NinjaOne here — so enforcement can ride that same pipe later. And we link their Splunk."
Say"Then we scan: we read the repos for agent configs and parse MCP manifests to inventory every agent and skill. Cloud-side, under a minute."
Land it: "Phase 1 has zero endpoint footprint. Nothing on a developer's laptop to break or disable. The Phase 2 runtime sensor deploys later through the RMM that's already linked — no re-onboarding."
Beat 2 — Discover~40s

Show the shadow AI

DO · Click "2 Discover"
Say"Here's BlueSky's full AI posture — 7 agents, 3 MCP servers, 41 skills, all scored."
SayPoint at the red banner."This one matters. browser-fetch is an MCP server running under a developer account that was never registered with you. Network egress, credential access. This is shadow AI — until this scan, nobody knew it existed."
Say"And you can see the rest — all three MCP servers, all seven agents — each with its disposition already decided."
Beat 3 — Score · THE differentiator~50s

The skill score + human control

DO · Click "3 Score"
Say"This is what nobody else does. CrowdStrike and Wiz tell you an agent is risky. We score every individual skill across 10 dimensions."
SayPoint at the gauge."browser-fetch scores 6.8. You see exactly why — network egress 9, supply chain 8, prompt injection 7. Not a black box."
Say"Under 3.0 auto-approved, so analysts never waste time on autocomplete. 8.0 and above is critical."
SayPoint at the HITL card."Here's the part you control. shell-exec-sudo at 9.8 is auto-blocked by policy — but env-reader at 9.2 is held: paused, with a critical notification pushed to you. You approve, keep holding, or block. A human decides before anything is enforced. You set that policy per client."
Land it: "Your team looks only at the middle band and stays in control of the critical edge. No black box blocking your client's developers behind your back."
Beat 4 — Splunk · for the Splunk shop~50s

Lands in their SIEM

DO · Click "4 Splunk"
Say"You already run Splunk. We don't ask you to leave it."
SayPoint at the setup card."One-time setup — point us at your HEC endpoint, pick the index. Every skill event then flows in CIM-normalized and MITRE-tagged."
SayPoint at the titled flow — "How a skill score becomes a Splunk alert"."The score raises that agent's risk in Splunk; when it crosses your threshold, Splunk fires an alert — in the model your analysts already use. From there, whatever automation you already run takes it: a SOAR playbook, a ticket, or just a Splunk alert action. If you've got a SOAR it slots straight in; if not, a Splunk alert is enough to start."
Land it: "We're a contributor to your SIEM, not a new pane of glass. That's the whole integration philosophy."
Beat 5 — Enforce~35s

Enforce via their RMM

DO · Click "5 Enforce"
Say"Detection is table stakes. Here's enforcement: block AI agents outside office hours, pushed through NinjaOne to all 14 endpoints. No new agent."
SayPoint at break-glass."The guardrail that keeps you out of trouble — a blocked developer requests access from their IDE, lands in your queue with context, one click to approve, and it sticks. Enforce without angry client calls."
Beat 6 — Audit · the budget unlock~30s

Audit-ready proof

DO · Click "6 Audit"
Say"Every blocked and enforced event becomes a signed, timestamped log — SOC 2 CC6.1, logical access controls."
Say"This is what you hand your client at the QBR: proof their AI agents are governed. It turns a security tool into a compliance line item — a second budget."
Say"Phase 1 does one standard properly. CMMC and ISO come in Phase 2."
Close~20s

The ask

Say"In four minutes: we found shadow AI you couldn't see, scored every skill, gave you a human checkpoint on the critical ones, fed it into your Splunk, enforced a policy through your RMM, and produced an audit artifact for your client — and nothing was installed on a single endpoint."
Say"We're taking a small number of Phase 1 design partners. I'd like an environment like yours to be one of them. Can we scope a pilot?"
Handling the obvious questions
"Is this shipping today?"
"Phase 1 is in build — 12-week plan, co-developed with design partners. The discovery and scoring you just saw ships first; enforcement follows in the same cycle."
"How is this different from CrowdStrike / Wiz?"
"They're built for an enterprise securing its own agents, on their platform, priced per enterprise. We're built for you — multi-tenant, riding your existing Splunk and RMM, per-tenant pricing, and we score at the skill level, not the asset level."
"What about false positives?"
"Conservative by default — auto-block only at 8.0+, and you can set critical actions to hold for your decision instead of blocking. Break-glass means a developer is never stuck. You tune the threshold per client."
"Which RMM / PSA / SIEM do you support?"
"Splunk, Sentinel, Elastic on the SIEM side; ConnectWise, Autotask, NinjaOne, Kaseya on RMM/PSA. Tell me your stack and that's the integration we build first for the pilot."
