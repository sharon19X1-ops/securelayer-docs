Phase 1 Demo — Talk Track

A beat-by-beat script for walking an MSSP prospect through the Phase 1 demo. ~4 minutes. Click the numbered beat rail at the top of the demo to advance.

6 beats · Connect → Discover → Score → Splunk → Enforce → Audit

▶ Open the demo ↗
Before you start · 10s

Frame it

"This is SecureLayer running as your MSSP console, managing one of your SMB clients — BlueSky Software. Nothing is installed on their machines. Let me show you what we find and what you can do about it."

"AI coding agents — Claude Code, Cursor, Copilot, MCP servers — are running in every client you manage. None of your current tools can see what they're allowed to do. That's the gap."

Beat 1 · Connect & Scan · 45s

Onboard a client

Click: "1 Connect"

"Onboarding a client takes minutes. You authorize read-only access to their GitHub org — OAuth, no credentials stored, nothing pushed to endpoints."

"We link the RMM you already run — NinjaOne here — so enforcement can ride that same pipe later. And we link their Splunk."

"Then we scan: we read the repos for agent configs and parse MCP manifests to inventory every agent and skill. Cloud-side, under a minute."

Land it: "Phase 1 has zero endpoint footprint. There's nothing on a developer's laptop to break or disable. The Phase 2 runtime sensor deploys later through the RMM that's already linked — no re-onboarding."
Beat 2 · Discover · 40s

Show the shadow AI

Click: "2 Discover"

"Here's BlueSky's AI posture — 7 agents, 3 MCP servers, 41 skills, all scored."

Point at the red banner: "This one matters. browser-fetch is an MCP server running under a developer account that was never registered with you. Network egress, credential access. This is shadow AI — until this scan, nobody knew it existed."

"Notice four skills are already auto-blocked. You didn't have to triage them."

Beat 3 · Score · 50s · THE differentiator

The skill score

Click: "3 Score"

"This is what nobody else does. CrowdStrike and Wiz tell you an agent is risky. We score every individual skill across 10 dimensions."

Point at the gauge: "browser-fetch scores 6.8. You see exactly why — network egress 9, supply chain 8, prompt injection 7. Not a black box."

"The policy is automatic: 8.0+ blocked before it runs — shell-exec-sudo at 9.8. Under 3.0 auto-approved, so analysts never waste time on autocomplete."

Land it: "Your team only ever looks at the middle band. That's the difference between an alert firehose and a queue a human can actually clear."
Beat 4 · Splunk · 50s · for the Splunk shop

Lands in their SIEM

Click: "4 Splunk"

"You already run Splunk. We don't ask you to leave it."

Point at the setup card: "One-time setup — point us at your HEC endpoint, pick the index. Every skill event then flows in CIM-normalized and MITRE-tagged."

Point at the flow: "The skill score becomes a risk contribution on the agent's risk object in your RBA. Crosses threshold, fires a risk notable — in the model your analysts already use. Then hands off to Torq."

Land it: "We're a contributor to your SIEM, not a new pane of glass. That's the whole integration philosophy."
Beat 5 · Enforce · 35s

Enforce via their RMM

Click: "5 Enforce"

"Detection is table stakes. Here's enforcement: block AI agents outside office hours, pushed through NinjaOne to all 14 endpoints. No new agent."

Point at break-glass: "The guardrail that keeps you out of trouble — a blocked developer requests access from their IDE, lands in your queue with context, one click to approve, and it sticks. Enforce without angry client calls."

Beat 6 · Audit · 30s · the budget unlock

Audit-ready proof

Click: "6 Audit"

"Every blocked and enforced event becomes a signed, timestamped log — SOC 2 CC6.1, logical access controls."

"This is what you hand your client at the QBR: proof their AI agents are governed. It turns a security tool into a compliance line item — a second budget."

"Phase 1 does one standard properly. CMMC and ISO come in Phase 2."

Close · 20s

The ask

"In four minutes: we found shadow AI you couldn't see, scored every skill, fed it into your Splunk, enforced a policy through your RMM, and produced an audit artifact for your client — and nothing was installed on a single endpoint."

"We're taking a small number of Phase 1 design partners. I'd like an environment like yours to be one of them. Can we scope a pilot?"

Handling the obvious questions
"Is this shipping today?"
"Phase 1 is in build — 12-week plan, co-developed with design partners. The discovery and scoring you just saw ships first; enforcement follows in the same cycle."
"How is this different from CrowdStrike / Wiz?"
"They're built for an enterprise securing its own agents, on their platform, priced per enterprise. We're built for you — multi-tenant, riding your existing Splunk and RMM, per-tenant pricing, and we score at the skill level, not the asset level."
"What about false positives?"
"Conservative by default — auto-block only at 8.0+, and break-glass means a developer is never stuck. You tune the threshold per client."
"Which RMM / PSA / SIEM do you support?"
"Splunk, Sentinel, Elastic on the SIEM side; ConnectWise, Autotask, NinjaOne, Kaseya on RMM/PSA. Tell me your stack and that's the integration we build first for the pilot."
