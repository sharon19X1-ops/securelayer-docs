4

Personalization Guide

Before Every Send

Spend 5 minutes on LinkedIn or the MSSP's website before sending. One personalized sentence in the first paragraph doubles reply rates. Replace the bracketed section below with what you find.

What to Look ForWhere to Find ItHow to Use It
Client industry focus MSSP website "Industries" or "Clients" page "Given your focus on [healthcare/legal/fintech] clients, the compliance angle here is particularly relevant…"
Recent blog post or webinar MSSP blog, LinkedIn posts "I read your post on [topic] — your framing on [X] is exactly the problem we're solving at the agent layer."
Current PSA / RMM stack LinkedIn job posts, partner badges on website "Since you're on ConnectWise, SecureLayer integrates natively — no new tooling to evaluate."
Team size / client count LinkedIn, Crunchbase, website Calibrate framing: <20 clients → "hands-on vCISO" framing; 50+ clients → "scale across your portfolio" framing
Recent hire or job post LinkedIn Jobs Hiring a "cloud security engineer"? "I saw you're expanding your cloud security practice — this fits that motion."

Do

  • Name the specific AI tools (Copilot, Cursor, Claude Code) — generic "AI" doesn't land
  • Reference the audit question — it's the one forcing function they already feel
  • Mention the demo URL — give them something to explore before a call
  • Ask for 20 minutes, not a demo — lower commitment = higher reply rate
  • Invite pushback — "even if only to tell me what we're getting wrong" disarms defensiveness
  • Send Tuesday–Thursday, 7–9am or 3–5pm prospect local time

Don't

  • Don't pitch the full product in email — the goal is the call, not the close
  • Don't use "I" as the first word — starts the email in the wrong frame
  • Don't say "revolutionary" or "game-changing" — kills credibility instantly
  • Don't attach the one-pager to the cold email — it triggers spam filters and feels presumptuous
  • Don't describe the product as "live" or "deployed" — it's a prototype; stealth framing protects this
  • Don't send more than 2 follow-ups — 3rd touch should be a LinkedIn message, not another email
5

Response Handling — What to Say When They Reply

After the Reply
REPLY TYPE: "Interested — let's talk"
Reply within 2 hours. Send a Calendly link with 3 specific slots. In the same reply, send the one-pager and the demo URL. Subject: Re: — looking forward to it, here's context ahead of our call
REPLY TYPE: "Send me more info first"
Don't send a deck. Send: (1) the one-pager PDF, (2) the demo URL, (3) one sentence: "Happy to walk through anything on a call — does [specific day] work?" Keep the call alive; don't let async kill the momentum.
REPLY TYPE: "Not the right person — try [Name]"
Gold. Reply: "Thank you — really appreciate the redirect." Then email [Name] using the referral variant (Template 3), opening with "[Original Contact] suggested I reach out." You've just converted a cold email into a warm intro.
REPLY TYPE: "We already handle this" or "Not a priority"
Don't argue. Ask one question: "Genuinely curious — what are you using today to govern AI agent behavior on client endpoints? I'm trying to understand the landscape." If they answer, you get competitive intelligence. If they don't, you've left the door open gracefully. Log the objection in your discovery notes.
